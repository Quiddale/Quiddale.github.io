About

That said, welcome to my page. I discovered coding in January 2017 so compared to the rest of the community I’m somewhat of a noob, but I’m learning. On June 26th 2017 I took my first step into a larger world when I started the Web Development Immersive course at General Assembly in London, Immersive being the optimum word. 12 gruelling weeks, all day, everyday (weekends too) of languages, command lines, editors, graphics, databases, tears, rapidly greying hairs and enough tea and coffee to sink a ship, all designed to give aspiring developers as idea of the breath of material used in the world of development.

To design, to think broadly, to invent, to speak and write, to make, to test, to fail, to question and debate, to stretch a definition and to solve problems beautifully. Two books book really set me on this path to wanting to code, Seymour Papert the inventor of Legos MindStorm and the Futurist Neal Stephenson. Due to these men I start to think of constructing a Metaverse that was a collective virtual shared space, created by the convergence of virtually enhanced physical reality and physically persistent virtual space, including the sum of all virtual worlds, augmented reality, and the internet. The core idea was to design a space where two children could collator and investigate these metaverse though Google cardboard.

The world metaverse combines the prefix “meta” meaning “beyond” with “universe” and is typically used to describe the concept of a future iteration of the internet, made up of persistent, shared, 3D virtual spaces linked into a perceived virtual universe.

So now here I am, a little older, a touch wiser, with knowledge I wouldn’t have believed possible when I started learning, there a few more miles in my legs due to cycling around Richmond Park. And to my family delight I been duly working my way through The Food Lab: Better Home Cooking Through Science by J. Kenji Lopez-Alt not sure why I would exchange one science for another but It was a lovely break from coding.
